Discovery of Radioactivity

Splitting the Secret of Matter // 1896

Discovery: Henri Becquerel Year: 1896 Contribution: Nuclear Science

In 1896, while investigating phosphorescence in uranium salts, Henri Becquerel accidentally discovered that uranium emitted a form of radiation that could pass through paper and fog photographic plates. This serendipitous discovery overturned the long-held belief that atoms were immutable and inactive.

"It was as if we had discovered a new light, one that shone from within the very heart of the earth."

The Curie Legacy

Following Becquerel's discovery, Marie and Pierre Curie began a rigorous search for other radioactive elements, eventually isolating polonium and radium. Their work earned them the Nobel Prize and laid the foundation for modern nuclear physics, medical imaging, and cancer treatment.

Medical Imaging

The discovery led directly to the development of X-rays and PET scans, revolutionizing how we see inside the human body.

Energy Frontier

Radioactivity provided the theoretical basis for nuclear energy, a field where France remains a global leader today.
