Camera Phone

The Birth of Social Media // 1997

Inventor: Philippe Kahn Year: 1997 Innovation: Image Sharing

In 1997, while in the waiting room for his daughter's birth, Philippe Kahn combined a digital camera with a cellular phone and a web server. He took the first-ever cell phone photo of his newborn daughter and instantly shared it with 2,000 family and friends.

"I wanted to share the moment, not just describe it."

The Social Revolution

Kahn's invention was not just about the hardware, but about the instant shareability of visual moments. This prototype proved that the future of mobile phones lay in becoming our primary social and visual capture devices.

Technical Proof

Kahn wrote the software himself while in the hospital, bridging the gap between mobile telecommunications and internet servers.

Cultural Legacy

This single moment in Santa Cruz, CA, empowered by French ingenuity, gave birth to the visual-first social media landscape we live in today.
