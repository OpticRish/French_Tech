The Stethoscope

Internal Acoustic Observation // 1816

Inventor: René Laennec Year: 1816 Field: Medical Diagnosis

In 1816, physician René Laennec was treating a patient with a heart condition. For reasons of modesty and the patient's build, he found traditional physical examination difficult. Recalling that sound travels through solids, he rolled a piece of paper into a tube and placed it against her chest.

"I was surprised and pleased to hear the beating of the heart much more clearly than I had ever done by direct application of the ear."

Engineering Sound

Laennec eventually replaced the paper tube with a wooden version, creating the first stethoscope (from the Greek stethos for "chest"). This allowed doctors to non-invasively listen to the internal sounds of the lungs and heart, creating a new branch of medicine called mediate auscultation.

Standardization

Laennec's invention transformed medicine from a subjective art to a more objective science based on internal physical signs.

Global Symbol

The stethoscope remains the universal symbol of the medical profession, a testament to the enduring power of Laennec's simple yet brilliant idea.
